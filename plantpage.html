The power of plants: How urban green spaces can improve our health, happiness and environment

Urban green spaces are areas where plants and other vegetation grow in cities. They can include parks, gardens, street trees, green roofs, and community gardens. Urban green spaces provide many benefits for people and the environment, improving mental health by reducing
  • stress, anxiety, and depression. Studies have shown that spending time in nature can boost mood, creativity, and cognitive function. Green spaces can also foster social interactions and community cohesion.
  • Enhancing physical health by promoting exercise, fresh air, and exposure to sunlight. Green spaces can encourage people to walk, bike, or play outdoors, which can lower the risk of obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular diseases. They can also provide natural shade and cooling, which can prevent heat-related illnesses.
  • Supporting biodiversity and ecosystem services by providing habitats for plants and animals, and filtering pollutants and carbon dioxide from the air. Green spaces can increase the diversity and abundance of urban wildlife, such as birds, insects, and mammals. They can also improve the quality of water, soil, and air by absorbing rainwater, reducing erosion, and producing oxygen.

Benefits of keeping indoor plants

Improving air quality by absorbing harmful pollutants and carbon dioxide, and releasing oxygen. Some indoor plants, like spider plants, peace lilies, and snake plants, are especially effective at filtering the air of volatile organic compounds (VOCs), which are emitted by paints, carpets, cleaners, and other sources.
Reducing stress levels by creating a calming and soothing atmosphere. Studies have shown that indoor plants can lower blood pressure, heart rate, and cortisol levels, and improve mood and cognitive function. They can also enhance your creativity and productivity by boosting your attention and memory.
Helping with healing and recovery by promoting a positive outlook and reducing pain perception.
